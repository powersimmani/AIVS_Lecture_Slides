GAN Inversion: From Images to Latent Codes
Enabling real image editing with pre-trained GANs
What is GAN Inversion?
Goal: Find latent code w such that G(w) = x (real image)

Why? Pre-trained GAN can only generate from random z. Inversion allows editing REAL images by manipulating their latent codes.
1. Optimization-based
Directly optimize latent code to reconstruct image.
Slow but accurate. Per-image optimization.
w* = argmin ||G(w) - x||2 + lambda * ||w - w_avg||2
2. Encoder-based
Train encoder E: x -> w (single forward pass).
Fast but less accurate. Examples: pSp, e4e
w = E(x) where E is trained to invert G
3. Hybrid
Encoder initialization + optimization refinement.
Best of both worlds. Used in practice.
MethodSpeedAccuracyEditability
OptimizationSlow (mins)HighMedium
EncoderFast (ms)MediumHigh
HybridMedium (sec)HighHigh
Applications
Face Editing: Age, expression, pose, lighting
Style Transfer: Apply artistic styles to photos
Restoration: Super-resolution, inpainting
Morphing: Interpolate between two faces
Attribute Manipulation: Add/remove glasses, hair color
