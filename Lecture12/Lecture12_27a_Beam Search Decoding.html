Beam Search: Better Decoding Strategy
Finding better sequences than greedy decoding
Concept
Problem: Greedy Decoding
Always picking the highest probability token at each step can miss the globally optimal sequence.
Solution: Beam Search
Keep track of k best candidates (beams) at each step. Expand all, keep top-k by cumulative probability.
Example (beam_width=3):

Step 1: "I" → [am(0.4), love(0.3), like(0.2)]
Step 2: Expand each → 9 candidates
  → Keep top 3: "I am", "I love", "I like"
Step 3: Continue until <EOS>
Final: Return highest scoring complete sequence
Trade-off: Larger beam = better quality but slower
Note: Beam search is not exhaustive; still approximate
Parameters & Settings
beam_width (k) Number of candidates to track. Typical: 4-6 for translation, 2-3 for dialogue.
length_penalty Adjusts preference for longer/shorter outputs. α > 1 favors longer, α < 1 favors shorter.
no_repeat_ngram Prevents repeating n-grams. Helps avoid repetitive text generation.
