The Challenge
Why Standard Distances Fail
Euclidean distance assumes point-to-point alignment.

Time series often have:
- Different lengths
- Phase shifts (same pattern, different timing)
- Speed variations (same pattern, different duration)
Solution: Elastic Distance Measures
Allow time axis warping to align similar patterns.
Find optimal alignment, then measure distance.
Key Methods
DTW (Dynamic Time Warping)
- Finds optimal alignment between two sequences
- Allows stretching/compressing time axis
- Complexity: O(n*m) with dynamic programming
- Most widely used for time series similarity
K-Shape
- Shape-based distance using cross-correlation
- Much faster than DTW: O(n log n)
- Shift-invariant comparison
- Good for general time series clustering
